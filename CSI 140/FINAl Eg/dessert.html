Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

  • 1 and 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350째F (175째C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round baking pans.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat on medium speed until well combined.
  4. Stir in the boiling water until the batter is smooth. The batter will be thin, but that's okay.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared baking pans.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool the cakes in the pans for 10 minutes, then remove them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Frost and decorate as desired.

Fruit Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup pastry cream (store-bought or homemade)
  • Assorted fresh fruits (strawberries, kiwi, blueberries, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup apricot jam
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and fit it into a tart pan. Trim any excess dough.
  3. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and line it with parchment paper. Fill with pie weights or dried beans.
  4. Bake the crust for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown. Remove the parchment paper and pie weights, and let the crust cool completely.
  5. Spread the pastry cream evenly over the cooled crust.
  6. Arrange the fresh fruits on top of the pastry cream in a decorative pattern.
  7. In a small saucepan, heat the apricot jam and water over low heat until melted. Brush the glaze over the fruit to give it a shiny finish.
  8. Refrigerate the tart for at least 1 hour before serving.
